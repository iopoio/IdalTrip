# Design System Document: High-End Editorial Travel Experience

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star: "The Digital Curator"

This design system is built to transform a standard travel utility into a premium editorial journey. Our Creative North Star is **"The Digital Curator"**—a philosophy that treats the mobile viewport not as a screen of buttons, but as a high-end physical magazine.

To break the "template" look typical of travel apps, we move away from rigid, boxed-in layouts. Instead, we utilize **intentional asymmetry**, **overlapping imagery**, and **aggressive white space**. The experience should feel like it was curated by an editor, not generated by an algorithm. By utilizing high-contrast typography and tonal layering, we guide the user's eye through a narrative, creating a sense of discovery rather than just "browsing."

---

## 2. Colors & Atmospheric Tones

Our palette moves beyond generic "app colors" into a sophisticated, breathing atmosphere. We use a base of warm off-whites (`surface`) paired with a deep, authoritative primary accent (`primary`).

### The Color Logic
*   **Surface Hierarchy:** We utilize the `surface-container` tiers to create depth. Avoid flat layouts.
*   **The "No-Line" Rule:** **Strictly prohibit 1px solid borders** for sectioning. Differentiation must be achieved through background shifts (e.g., a `surface-container-low` card resting on a `surface` background) or generous white space.
*   **The "Glass & Gradient" Rule:** For floating navigation or top-level overlays, use Glassmorphism. Apply a backdrop-blur (16px–24px) to `surface` colors at 80% opacity. 
*   **Signature Textures:** For high-impact CTAs, use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (#a63415) to `primary_container` (#c84c2b) to add "soul" and depth.

| Token | Hex | Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `primary` | #a63415 | Signature "Sunset Orange" for CTAs and highlights. |
| `surface` | #faf9f7 | Soft off-white "Paper" background. |
| `on_surface` | #1a1c1b | Deep charcoal for maximum legibility and authority. |
| `secondary` | #4f6073 | Muted "Ocean Grey" for secondary actions and meta-info. |
| `surface_container_low` | #f4f3f1 | Subtle nesting for grouped content. |

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice

We pair the modern, clean Korean readability of **Pretendard** with the sophisticated, timeless elegance of **Noto Serif KR**. This contrast is the cornerstone of our "Magazine Feel."

*   **Display & Headlines (`notoSerif`):** Use these for destination names and article titles. The high-contrast serif evokes a sense of history and luxury.
*   **UI & Body (`plusJakartaSans` / `Pretendard`):** Used for navigation, descriptions, and functional elements. It provides a modern, high-tech counterpoint to the serif headers.

### Scale Strategy
*   **Display-LG (56px):** Use for hero moments. Let it bleed off-center for an asymmetrical editorial look.
*   **Headline-MD (28px):** The standard for article titles.
*   **Body-MD (14px):** Optimized for long-form reading with a line-height of 1.6 to ensure "breathing room."
*   **Label-SM (11px):** Uppercase with increased letter-spacing for category tags (e.g., "STAY", "DINING").

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering

Traditional shadows and borders create "visual noise." This system uses **Tonal Layering** to convey hierarchy.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Stack `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) elements on top of `surface` (#faf9f7) backgrounds to create a soft, natural lift.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** When a shadow is required for a floating CTA or a primary card, use a custom shadow: `0px 12px 32px rgba(26, 28, 27, 0.06)`. The shadow color must be a tint of `on_surface`, never pure black.
*   **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If a boundary is required for accessibility, use the `outline_variant` (#e0bfb8) at 20% opacity. Never use a 100% opaque border.
*   **Asymmetric Radii:** To feel more custom, consider using `xl` (12px) rounding on three corners and `none` (0px) on one corner for featured images to create a "tabbed" magazine look.

## 5. Components

### Cards & Photo-Rich Containers
*   **Editorial Card:** Full-bleed imagery with a `display-sm` title overlapping the image slightly. No dividers.
*   **The "No-Divider" Rule:** Vertical white space (32px, 48px, or 64px) is the only allowed separator for list items.

### Navigation & Chips
*   **Horizontally Scrollable Chips:** Use `surface_container_high` with `label-md` text. Roundedness: `full`. For the selected state, transition to `primary` with `on_primary` text.
*   **The Signature Timeline:** For itineraries, use a 1px `outline_variant` vertical line with significant padding. Use `primary` dots for active stops and `secondary_fixed_dim` for past stops.

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** Gradient of `primary` to `primary_container`. No border. High-contrast `on_primary` text.
*   **Secondary:** `surface_container_highest` background with `on_surface` text.
*   **Tertiary:** Text-only with a 2px underline of `primary` to emphasize the "editorial link" look.

## 6. Do’s and Don'ts

### Do
*   **Do** allow images to break the grid. A photo that spans 100% width next to a text block with 24px margins creates an expensive, editorial feel.
*   **Do** use extreme scale. A very large `display-lg` headline next to a very small `label-sm` category tag creates sophisticated contrast.
*   **Do** use `surface_bright` for interactive states to create a "glow" effect.

### Don’t
*   **Don’t** use 1px solid borders to separate content. It makes the app look like a data table, not a magazine.
*   **Don’t** crowd the screen. If you feel like you need more content, you probably need more white space instead.
*   **Don’t** use generic system icons. Use light-weight (1.5pt stroke) custom icons that match the "Ocean Grey" (`secondary`) tone.
*   **Don't** use pure black (#000000). Always use `on_surface` (#1a1c1b) for a more premium, "ink-on-paper" feel.
